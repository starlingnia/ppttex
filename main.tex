\begin{frame}
\myheading{Symmetry of QCD}
\vfill
QCD: \hfill
 $\mathcal{Z} =
\int \mathcal{D} A \mathcal{D}\bar{q} \mathcal{D}qe^{-
S}$, Gauge Boson+Fermion\hfill\null

\vspace{1em}{
\centering
$S =
\int d^4x \left[ - \frac{1}{2g^2} \mathrm{Tr} F^{\mu\nu} F_{\mu\nu}
+ \bar q_a (i \slashed{D} - m_a) q_a \right]$, $a$ in
[1,$N_f$]
}
\vspace{1em}

Specifically,in the up-down quark mass zero limit,

Flavored chiral symmetry $SU(2)_L \times SU(2)_R$ :
\[
    q \to \exp(i\alpha_a T^a + i\beta_a T^a \gamma_5)q, \quad \bar{q} \to \bar{q} \exp(-i\alpha_a T^a + i\beta_a T^a \gamma_5),
\]

$U(1)_A$ Symmetry :
\[
    q \to \exp(i\epsilon\gamma_5)q, \quad \bar{q} \to \bar{q} \exp(i\epsilon\gamma_5),
\]

\vfill
\end{frame}

% ==============================================================================
\begin{frame}
\myheading{ Meson Correlation Functions}
\vfill
We consider the
following set of meson operators
\begin{gather*}
    O_\Gamma(x) = \bar{q}(x)\Gamma F^aq(x), \\[2ex]
    \Gamma \in \left\{ \mathbf{1}(S), \gamma_5(P), \gamma_\mu(V), \gamma_\mu\gamma_5(A), \sigma_{\mu\nu} = \frac{i}{2}[\gamma_\mu, \gamma_\nu](T), \sigma_{\mu\nu}\gamma_5(X) \right\}
\end{gather*}

And we measure the two-points correlation function of the meson operators of different channels.
Accroding to the symmetry properties, the asymptotic behavior of the 2-point correlation function at large distances should be approximately be $A \rm{Cosh}(m_\Gamma(x-L/2)) $
\vfill
\end{frame}

% ==============================================================================
% Slide 8b: Domain-Wall Fermions (Domain-Wall 费米子介绍页)
% ==============================================================================
\begin{frame}
\myheading{Domain-Wall Fermions (DWF)}
\vfill
\setbeamerfont{itemize/enumerate body}{size=\fontsize{9.5pt}{11.5pt}\selectfont}
\fontsize{9.5pt}{11.5pt}\selectfont
\begin{itemize}
    \item 5D Domain-Wall Realization:
    DWF realizes chiral fermions as states localized on the boundaries by adding a 5th dimension.

    \item 5D Möbius Domain Wall Operator:
    \[ D^{\text{5D}}_{s,s'}(m) = D_{+}^{(s)}\delta_{s,s'} + D_{-}^{(s)} (P_+ \delta_{s-1,s'} + P_- \delta_{s+1,s'}) - m (D_{-}^{(1)} P_+ \delta_{s,1}\delta_{s',L_s} + D_{-}^{(L_s)} P_- \delta_{s,L_s}\delta_{s',1}) \]
    where $D_{+}^{(s)} = b_5 D_W + 1$, $D_{-}^{(s)} = c_5 D_W - 1$, $P_\pm = \frac{1 \pm \gamma_5}{2}$. Here, $b=1.5,\ c=0.5$.

    \item Residual Mass and Chiral Breaking:
    At finite $L_s$, chiral symmetry is slightly broken by the residual mass:
    \[ \overline{\psi} \Delta_L \psi \simeq m_{\mathrm{res}} \overline{\psi} \psi + O(\psi, \bar \psi, A) + \mathcal{O}(a^2) \]
\end{itemize}
\vfill
\end{frame}

% Slide 13: Extracting Screening Masses: Methodology (抽取筛选质量：方法学)
% ==============================================================================
\begin{frame}
\myheading{Extracting Screening Masses: Methodology}
\vfill
\setbeamerfont{itemize/enumerate body}{size=\fontsize{9pt}{12.5pt}\selectfont}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Screening Mass Fit: The physical screening mass $m_{\text{scr}}$ is extracted by performing a correlated $\chi^2$ fit of $C(x)$ to the asymptotic form:
    \[
        C(x) = A \cosh\left(m_{\text{scr}}\left(x - \frac{L}{2}\right)\right)
    \]

    \item Local Effective Mass: The local effective mass $m_{\text{eff}}(x)$ is solved numerically to identify a suitable plateau, which is used to determine the fitting range $[x_{\text{min}}, x_{\text{max}}]$:
    \[
        \frac{C(x)}{C(x + 1)} = \frac{\cosh(m_{\text{eff}}(x)(x - L/2))}{\cosh(m_{\text{eff}}(x)(x + 1 - L/2))}
    \]

    \item Statistical Error: Jackknife method and Binning analysis are used to handle statistical uncertainties and eliminate HMC autocorrelation bias.
\end{itemize}
\vfill
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}[noframenumbering]
\myheading{Backup: Overlap Dirac Operator}
\vfill
The massless operator $D_{\mathrm{ov}}(0) = \frac{1}{2} (1 + \gamma_5 \mathrm{sgn}(H_W))$ with Hermitian $H_W$ satisfies the Ginsparg-Wilson relation:
\[ \gamma_5 D_{\mathrm{ov}} + D_{\mathrm{ov}} \gamma_5 = 2 D_{\mathrm{ov}} \gamma_5 D_{\mathrm{ov}} \]
The massive overlap operator is defined as:
\[ D_{\mathrm{ov}}(m) = D_{\mathrm{ov}}(0) + m (1 - D_{\mathrm{ov}}(0)) = \frac{1+m}{2} + \frac{1-m}{2} \gamma_5 \mathrm{sgn}(H_W) \]
\vfill
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}[noframenumbering]
\myheading{Backup: Order of Chiral Phase Transition}
\vfill
\setbeamerfont{itemize/enumerate body}{size=\fontsize{9.5pt}{11.5pt}\selectfont}
\fontsize{9.5pt}{11.5pt}\selectfont
\begin{itemize}
    \item Perturbative $4-\epsilon$ expansion RG analysis of effective $\Phi^4$ linear sigma model shows no stable IR fixed point for $N_f > \sqrt{3}$ flavor symmetry {\scriptsize [Pisarski-Wilczek (1983)]}.
    \item For $N_f \geq 3$, the anomaly effect doesn't change the order of the transition; for $N_f=2$, it becomes a quadratic term which can smooth out the singularity and make the transition 2nd-order.
    \item If the anomaly effect still exists, the transition can be $\mathrm{O}(4)$ class.
    \item Higher-loop/FRG computations suggest other possibilities {\scriptsize [Vicari et al. 2005, Fejos 2022]} and Conformal Bootstrap indicates interesting points {\scriptsize [Nakayama-Ohtsuki 2016]}.
\end{itemize}
\vfill
\end{frame}

Symmetry restoration behavior of $U(1)_A$ and flavor $SU(2)_L\times SU(2)_R$ determines the universality class:
