\myheading{Symmetry of QCD}
\vfill
QCD: \hfill
 $\mathcal{Z} =
\int \mathcal{D} A \mathcal{D}\bar{q} \mathcal{D}qe^{-
S}$, Gauge Boson+Fermion\hfill\null

\vspace{1em}
$S =
\int d^4x \left[ - \frac{1}{2g^2} \mathrm{Tr} F^{\mu\nu} F_{\mu\nu}
+ \bar q_a (i \slashed{D} - m_a) q_a \right]$, $a$ in
[1,$N_f$]

\vspace{1em}
Specifically,in the up-down quark mass zero limit:

Flavored chiral symmetry $SU(2)_L \times SU(2)_R$ :
\[
    q \to \exp(i\alpha_a T^a + i\beta_a T^a \gamma_5)q, \quad \bar{q} \to \bar{q} \exp(-i\alpha_a T^a + i\beta_a T^a \gamma_5),
\]

$U(1)_A$ Symmetry :
\[
    q \to \exp(i\epsilon\gamma_5)q, \quad \bar{q} \to \bar{q} \exp(i\epsilon\gamma_5),
\]

We use the mass difference of the screening masses of different channels to
estimate the degree of the symmetry breaking.
\vfill
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}
\myheading{ Meson Correlation Functions}
\vfill
We consider the
following set of meson operators
\begin{gather*}
    O_\Gamma(x) = \bar{q}(x)\Gamma F^aq(x), \\[2ex]
    \Gamma \in \left\{ \mathbf{1}(S), \gamma_5(P), \gamma_\mu(V), \gamma_\mu\gamma_5(A), \sigma_{\mu\nu} = \frac{i}{2}[\gamma_\mu, \gamma_\nu](T), \sigma_{\mu\nu}\gamma_5(X) \right\}
\end{gather*}

And we measure the two-points correlation function of the meson operators of different channels.
Accroding to the symmetry properties, the asymptotic behavior of the 2-point correlation function at large distances should be approximately be $a \rm{Cosh}(m_\Gamma(x-L/2)) $
\vfill
\end{frame}

\setbeamerfont{itemize/enumerate body}{size=\fontsize{11pt}{14pt}\selectfont}
\fontsize{11pt}{14pt}\selectfont
\vfill
\begin{itemize}
    \item Perturbative $4-\epsilon$ expansion Renormalization Group Analysis of effective $\Phi^4$ linear sigma model for $\langle \bar{q} P_L q \rangle$. They found for $N_f > \sqrt{3}$ flavor symmetry, there's no stable IR fixed point. {\scriptsize [Pisarski-Wilczek (1983)]} \\
    \item %The anomaly effect term is a determinant term in the effective linear sigma model, which is a $N_f$-th order term. 
    For $N_f \geq 3 $ flavor case, the anomaly effect doesn't change the order of the transition;But for $N_f = 2$ flavor case, it becomes a quadratic term, which can smooth out the singularity and change the transition to second order.  \\
   \item  If the anomaly effect still exists, the transition can be $\mathrm{O}(4)$ class. If the anomaly effect is  suppressed and  $\mathrm{U}(1)_{\mathrm{A}} $ symmetry is restored before or at the critical temperature $T_c$, then transition should be still first order
\item But recently higher-loop computations and FRG computations suggested different possibilities {\scriptsize [E.Vicari ,F.Basile, A.Pelissetto 2005 , G. Fejos 2022]}, and Conformal Boostrap results indicate some interesting points too{\scriptsize [Nakayama Ohtsuki 2016}

    q &\to \exp(i\Sigma^i\epsilon_i)q, \\

\end{align*}

where $\Sigma^i$ has three components,

\[
    \Sigma^i = (\gamma_k, -i\gamma_5\gamma_k, \gamma_5), \quad k = 1, 2.

% ==============================================================================
% Slide 8b: Domain-Wall Fermions (Domain-Wall 费米子介绍页)
% ==============================================================================
\begin{frame}
\myheading{Domain-Wall Fermions (DWF)}
\vfill
\setbeamerfont{itemize/enumerate body}{size=\fontsize{9.5pt}{11.5pt}\selectfont}
\fontsize{9.5pt}{11.5pt}\selectfont
\begin{itemize}
    \item {Overlap Dirac Operator}:
    The massless operator $D_{\mathrm{ov}}(0) = \frac{1}{2} (1 + \gamma_5 \mathrm{sgn}(H_W))$ satisfies the Ginsparg-Wilson relation:
    \[ \gamma_5 D_{\mathrm{ov}} + D_{\mathrm{ov}} \gamma_5 = 2 D_{\mathrm{ov}} \gamma_5 D_{\mathrm{ov}} \]
    The massive overlap operator is defined as:
    \[ D_{\mathrm{ov}}(m) = D_{\mathrm{ov}}(0) + m (1 - D_{\mathrm{ov}}(0)) = \frac{1+m}{2} + \frac{1-m}{2} \gamma_5 \mathrm{sgn}(H_W) \]
    
    \item {5D Domain-Wall Realization}:
    DWF approximates the sign function $\mathrm{sgn}(H_W)$ by introducing a 5th dimension of size $L_s$. The effective 4D operator is:
    \[ D^{\text{4D}}_{\mathrm{DW}} (m) = \frac{1+ m}{2} + \frac{1 - m}{2} \gamma_5 \tanh (L_s \tanh^{- 1} (H_M)) \]
    where $H_M = \gamma_5 \frac{2 D_W}{2 + D_W}$. As $L_s \to \infty$, it converges exponentially to the exact overlap operator $D_{\mathrm{ov}}(m)$.
\end{itemize}
\vfill
\end{frame}

% Slide 13: Extracting Screening Masses: Methodology (抽取筛选质量：方法学)
% ==============================================================================
\begin{frame}
\myheading{Extracting Screening Masses: Methodology}
\vfill
\setbeamerfont{itemize/enumerate body}{size=\fontsize{9pt}{12.5pt}\selectfont}
\begin{itemize}
    \item {Local Effective Mass}: Numerically solve the ratio at each spatial coordinate $x$ to obtain the local effective mass $m_{\text{eff}}(x)$:
    \[
        \frac{C(x)}{C(x + 1)} = \frac{\cosh(m_{\text{eff}}(x)(x - L/2))}{\cosh(m_{\text{eff}}(x)(x + 1 - L/2))}
    \]

    \item {Plateau and Range}: The plateau of $m_{\text{eff}}(x)$ at large separations is used to determine a suitable fitting range $[x_{\text{min}}, x_{\text{max}}]$.

    \item {Screening Mass Fit}: The physical screening mass $m_{\text{scr}}$ is extracted by performing a correlated $\chi^2$ fit of $C(x)$ to the asymptotic form:
    \[
        C(x) = A \cosh\left(m_{\text{scr}}\left(x - \frac{L}{2}\right)\right)
    \]

    \item {Statistical Error}: Jackknife method and Binning analysis are used to handle statistical uncertainties and eliminate HMC autocorrelation bias.
\end{itemize}
\vfill
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}
\myheading{Summary and Outlook}
%\setbeamerfont{itemize/enumerate body}{size=\fontsize{8.5pt}{11pt}\selectfont}
\vfill
\vspace{0.5ex}
\begin{itemize}
    \item {Physical Point}: Crossover transition with $T_c \approx 158$ MeV. Above $T_c$, chiral and $U(1)_A$ symmetry breakings are consistent with zero within errors.
    \item {Chiral Limit Study}: Extrapolations to $am_l \to 0$ support simultaneous restoration of chiral flavor and $U(1)_A$ symmetries (with $T_c \approx 165$ MeV).
    \item {Chiral-Spin Symmetry}: Remains broken up to 220 MeV, indicating active non-perturbative chromomagnetic interactions in QGP.
    \item {Transition Scenario}:
        \begin{itemize}
            \item {Previous}: 1st-order if $U(1)_A$ restored; 2nd-order $O(4)$ if $U(1)_A$ remains broken.
            \item {New}: $O(4)$ if $U(1)_A$ remains; weak 1st-order, or 2nd-order ($O(4)\times Z_2$, $O(4)\times Z_4$, $O(4)\times O(2)$) if $U(1)_A$ restored.
        \end{itemize}
    The symmetry restoration behavior of $U(1)_A$ and flavor $SU(2)_L\times SU(2)_R$ will determine the final universality class
\end{itemize}
\vspace{0.5em}
\end{frame}
